Focaccia Bread

A crispy, airy Italian classic

Recipe Details

  • Cooking Type: Bread / Baking

Ingredients

Starter (Poolish)

  • 200 gr bread flour
  • 200 gr water
  • 1/2 tsp yeast
  • 1 tsp honey or sugar

Dough

  • All the poolish
  • 430 gr bread flour
  • 304 gr very cold water
  • 16 gr salt
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

Additional

  • 9x13 inch dark metal pan
  • Flaky salt, generous amount
  • Fresh rosemary (optional)

Instructions

  1. The day before, make the starter by mixing bread flour, water, yeast, and honey. Cover and let rest on the countertop for about 5–6 hours.
  2. Once the poolish has tripled in volume and is bubbly, mix in the remaining flour, ice cold water, and salt. Add the olive oil. No kneading required.
  3. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes. Do 1 round of stretch and folds: grab the dough, stretch it out, and fold toward the center. Flip seam-side down and shape into a ball. The dough will be very wet and sticky.
  4. Place in the fridge overnight.
  5. The next day, line the pan with parchment paper and drizzle generously with olive oil. Shape the dough into a ball and place seam-side down in the pan. Grease the top with olive oil and cover with plastic wrap.
  6. Let rest in a warm place for 3.5–4 hours, until the dough covers the entire surface of the pan.
  7. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  8. Grease your fingertips, drizzle more oil on the focaccia, and dimple the surface. Sprinkle with flaky salt and fresh rosemary if using.
  9. Bake on the bottom rack for 10–12 minutes until the bottom is crispy.
  10. Move to the middle rack and bake another 5–7 minutes until golden and crispy on top.
  11. Remove from the oven, transfer to a baking rack without the parchment paper, and let cool for 20 minutes before cutting.
